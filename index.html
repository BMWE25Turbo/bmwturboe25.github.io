BMW Turbo (E25) - 1972 Prototype

Poster Origin: Likely BMW Museum Gift Shop (Late 1970s–80s)
Print Style: Rare dry-mounted German issue
Known Copies: 3 worldwide (U.S., Germany, Canada), but there very well probably are a few more in existence
Poster Dimensions: ~94 × 63 cm (with frame)

The Story

This is the BMW Turbo E25. Only two were ever built. It’s the concept that led to the M1 — mid-engine, gullwing doors, wild for its time. This poster was probably a BMW Museum gift shop print from the 70s or 80s, professionally mounted back when dry-mounting was the norm. I’ve only seen two others online — one in Germany, one in Ontario, Canada. You won’t find it on Amazon, you won’t find it on forums — it basically doesn’t exist unless you already have one. Total garage sale fluke. It’s one of the rarest things I own.

Turbo E25 Fast Facts

  • Designed by: Paul Bracq
  • Debut: 1972 Munich Olympics
  • Engine: Turbocharged inline-4
  • Layout: Mid-engine, rear-wheel drive
  • Doors: Gullwing
  • Top Speed: ~155 mph (250 km/h)
  • Legacy: Direct inspiration for the BMW M1
